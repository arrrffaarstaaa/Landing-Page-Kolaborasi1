OBSERVATION OF WIRE WASTE INTO BONSAI

Waste is the residue or discarded material from a production process, consumption, or other activity that cannot be used in its original form. Well, based on my observations, I will explain about wire waste. Wire is considered inorganic waste and has economic value when processed into handicrafts.

Wire is a material made of metal that is shaped into a long, thin cross-section. Wire is typically used for construction, electronics, and industry, and can also be used to make various crafts. Furthermore, wire waste can be divided into three parts: the wire body, the wire winding, and the wire surface.

Wire waste is also the remnant of unused metal waste and can pollute the environment if not properly utilized. Wire waste can damage ecosystems and disrupt the beauty of the surrounding nature. Therefore, we must use wire waste wisely.

The benefits of wire waste for humans include saving natural resources, reducing costs, and turning it into a business opportunity (crafts). The benefits of wire waste for nature are to reduce pollution (land, water, air), reduce environmental impact, reduce waste, and also preserve the environment.

OBSERVATION OF WIRE WASTE INTO BONSAI

Waste is a material produced from a process or activity that no longer has economic value or function for the intended recipient. Waste can negatively impact the environment and health if not properly managed. Therefore, effective waste management is crucial to reduce these negative impacts. As observed, I will explain about wire waste.

Wire waste is a type of wire waste often generated by industries, workshops, and households. Wire has the potential to pollute the environment if not managed properly. Wire waste can be copper wire, iron wire, or other types of wire that have economic value if reprocessed.

Effective wire waste management can help reduce environmental impacts and improve resource efficiency. Wire waste recycling can be achieved by collecting, sorting, and processing wire into raw materials that can be reused in production. This way, wire waste can be converted into a valuable resource and reduce the need for new raw materials.

The wire waste recycling process can also help reduce costs and conserve natural resources. Furthermore, proper wire waste management can help reduce the risk of environmental pollution and raise public awareness about the importance of responsible waste management.

Therefore, effective wire waste management not only benefits the environment but can also provide economic benefits to the community and industry. Therefore, efforts are needed to increase public awareness and participation in proper and responsible wire waste management.

OBSERVATION OF WIRE WASTE PROCESSING INTO BONSAI

Waste is the discarded product of an activity or production process. Many types of waste can be transformed into valuable items, one of which is wire waste. Wire waste can be processed into various crafts from unused wire. This material can be found in various places, from industrial areas and construction sites to households. Wire waste can consist of scraps of copper wire, steel wire, aluminum wire, or other types of wire that no longer have their primary functional value. Without proper management, this waste can accumulate and cause environmental problems.

This wire waste is often disposed of carelessly or mixed with other types of waste, making its assessment difficult. Accumulated wire waste can create an unsightly appearance, potentially injuring people or animals, and can also cause soil pollution if corrosion occurs. Therefore, we must process this waste into high-value products. This waste must also be utilized properly to avoid leaving small pieces that could cause harm.

Waste wire has a fairly hard texture, so making wire bonsai requires extra force to secure it. In the process of utilizing this waste, we can do so by applying the 3R principle (Recycle, Reduce, Reuse) to reduce waste and pollution. Waste and pollution can have a negative impact on society, therefore we must adhere to this principle. By applying the 3R principle, wire waste can be processed into aesthetically pleasing and functional products, such as wire bonsai and other products.

Overall, wire waste is not useless. With creativity and proper processing, it can become a valuable resource. Reusing wire waste is not only an effort to preserve the environment but also to improve the community's economy. My goal in creating this product (wire bonsai) is because wire has high aesthetic value and is attractive.
